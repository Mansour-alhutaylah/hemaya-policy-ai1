THIRD PARTY CYBERSECURITY STANDARD COMPLIANCE POLICY
Version 2.1 | Effective: January 2024

1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This policy establishes cybersecurity requirements for all third-party relationships with
Saudi Aramco. It applies to all personnel, contractors, and subcontractors who access
Saudi Aramco systems, networks, or data.

2. ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (TPC-1)

Third Party must establish, maintain, and communicate a Cybersecurity Acceptable Use
Policy (AUP) governing use of technology assets. All personnel must sign and acknowledge
the AUP annually. The AUP prohibits unauthorized use, sharing of credentials, and
installation of unauthorized software.

3. CYBERSECURITY ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES (TPC-2, TPC-3)

A designated Cybersecurity Officer is responsible for implementing and maintaining all
cybersecurity controls. The Cybersecurity Officer reports to senior management quarterly
on the status of the cybersecurity program. All third-party employees must complete
cybersecurity awareness training within 30 days of hire and annually thereafter.

4. ASSET MANAGEMENT (TPC-5)

An inventory of all technology assets including hardware, software, and data stores is
maintained and reviewed quarterly. Assets are classified according to sensitivity.
Unauthorized hardware and software are not permitted on Saudi Aramco networks.

5. ACCESS CONTROL

5.1 User Access Management (TPC-7)
Access to systems and applications is granted based on the principle of least privilege.
Access requests must be approved by the asset owner and the Cybersecurity Officer.
Access rights are reviewed quarterly and terminated immediately upon departure.

5.2 Privileged Access (TPC-8)
Privileged accounts are strictly controlled. A formal request and approval process
is required for all privileged access. Privileged access sessions are logged and
monitored. Shared privileged accounts are prohibited.

5.3 Remote Access (TPC-9)
Remote access to Saudi Aramco systems requires multi-factor authentication (MFA).
Remote sessions must use encrypted VPN connections. Remote access logs are reviewed
monthly for anomalies.

6. DATA SECURITY

6.1 Data Classification (TPC-24)
Information is classified into four levels: Public, Internal, Confidential, and
Restricted. All data must be labeled according to its classification. Handling
procedures are defined for each classification level.

6.2 Data Encryption (TPC-25)
Data classified as Confidential or Restricted must be encrypted at rest using AES-256
and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher. Encryption keys are managed by the
Cybersecurity Officer and rotated annually.

7. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT (TPC-42)

Systems are scanned for vulnerabilities monthly using approved scanning tools.
Critical vulnerabilities must be remediated within 15 days. High vulnerabilities
must be remediated within 30 days. Vulnerability reports are provided to Saudi Aramco
upon request.

8. INCIDENT RESPONSE (TPC-60)

A documented incident response plan is maintained and tested annually. Security
incidents affecting Saudi Aramco data or systems must be reported to Saudi Aramco
within 24 hours of detection. Post-incident reviews are conducted and reports shared.

9. COMPLIANCE REVIEW

Compliance with this policy is reviewed annually by internal audit. Findings are
reported to the Cybersecurity Officer and senior management for remediation tracking.
