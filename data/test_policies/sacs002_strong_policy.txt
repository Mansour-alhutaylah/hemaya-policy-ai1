SACS-002 THIRD PARTY CYBERSECURITY COMPLIANCE POLICY
Classification: Confidential | Version 3.0 | Effective: March 2024
Owner: Chief Information Security Officer | Review Cycle: Annual

=============================================================================
SECTION A — GENERAL REQUIREMENTS (All Third Parties)
=============================================================================

1. ACCEPTABLE USE POLICY (TPC-1)

Third Party MUST establish, maintain, and communicate a Cybersecurity Acceptable Use
Policy (AUP) governing the use of Third Party Technology Assets. The AUP must be
signed by all users prior to access, reviewed annually, and updated to address new
threat vectors. Violations of the AUP result in immediate access revocation and
disciplinary action up to and including termination.

2. CYBERSECURITY PROGRAM (TPC-2)

A formal cybersecurity program is established and documented, encompassing governance,
risk management, technical controls, and compliance monitoring. The program is reviewed
and approved by senior management annually. Program objectives align with Saudi Aramco's
third-party cybersecurity requirements and NIST CSF principles.

3. CYBERSECURITY ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES (TPC-3)

A designated Cybersecurity Officer (CSO) is appointed with documented accountability
for all cybersecurity controls. Role descriptions for all cybersecurity functions are
documented, reviewed, and approved annually. The CSO has authority to enforce controls,
escalate risks to executive leadership, and mandate remediation timelines.

4. CYBERSECURITY AWARENESS AND TRAINING (TPC-4)

All personnel must complete role-based cybersecurity awareness training within 5 days
of hire and every 12 months thereafter. Training completion is tracked and reported
quarterly to the CSO. Personnel with privileged access receive enhanced training
covering social engineering, phishing, and insider threat recognition. Training records
are retained for a minimum of 3 years.

5. ASSET MANAGEMENT AND INVENTORY (TPC-5)

A comprehensive inventory of all technology assets — including hardware, software,
licensed applications, cloud services, and data repositories — is maintained and
updated upon any change event. The inventory is reviewed and reconciled quarterly.
Assets are classified according to sensitivity and criticality using a four-tier
classification scheme: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted.

6. CYBERSECURITY RISK ASSESSMENT (TPC-6)

A formal risk assessment is conducted annually and upon significant changes to the
environment. The assessment identifies, evaluates, and prioritizes cybersecurity risks.
Risk treatment decisions — accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid — are documented and
approved by senior management. Risk registers are reviewed quarterly and updated as
new risks emerge.

7. ACCESS CONTROL — USER IDENTITY MANAGEMENT (TPC-7)

Access to all systems and applications is governed by the principle of least privilege.
All access requests must be approved by the data owner and the CSO. User access rights
are formally reviewed quarterly and immediately revoked upon role change or departure.
Shared accounts are prohibited. Each user maintains a unique identifier and access
logs are retained for a minimum of 12 months.

8. PRIVILEGED ACCESS MANAGEMENT (TPC-8)

Privileged accounts — including local administrator, service accounts, and root access
— are strictly controlled. A formal approval workflow is required for all privileged
access grants. Privileged sessions are recorded and monitored. Privileged account
credentials are rotated every 90 days. Standing privileged access is prohibited; just-
in-time access is used where technically feasible.

9. REMOTE ACCESS SECURITY (TPC-9)

All remote access to Saudi Aramco systems requires multi-factor authentication (MFA)
using TOTP or hardware tokens. Remote sessions are established exclusively via encrypted
VPN using TLS 1.3 or IPSec. Session timeouts are enforced at 15 minutes of inactivity.
Remote access logs are reviewed weekly for anomalies and suspicious activity.

10. NETWORK SEGMENTATION (TPC-10)

Production, development, and test environments are segregated by network segment.
Saudi Aramco-connected systems are isolated in a dedicated network zone with controlled
ingress and egress. Firewall rules are reviewed quarterly and unused rules are removed.
All firewall rule changes require a documented change request and CSO approval.

11. SECURITY MONITORING AND LOGGING (TPC-20)

Security events are logged for all critical systems including authentication events,
privileged access, configuration changes, and data access. Logs are centralized in a
SIEM solution, retained for a minimum of 12 months, and reviewed daily. Automated
alerting is configured for high-severity events with escalation to the CSO within 1 hour.

=============================================================================
SECTION A — DATA SECURITY AND INFORMATION MANAGEMENT
=============================================================================

12. DATA CLASSIFICATION AND HANDLING (TPC-24)

All data is classified into four levels: Public, Internal, Confidential, and Restricted.
Classification is applied at the point of creation and maintained throughout the data
lifecycle. Handling procedures — including storage, transmission, sharing, and disposal
— are defined and enforced for each classification level. Data owners are responsible for
ensuring correct classification and handling.

13. DATA ENCRYPTION (TPC-25)

Data classified Confidential or Restricted must be encrypted at rest using AES-256
and in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher. Encryption key management procedures are
documented, reviewed annually, and keys are rotated every 12 months. Encryption
exceptions require documented risk acceptance signed by the CSO.

14. SECURE DATA DISPOSAL (TPC-26)

All media and storage devices containing Saudi Aramco data must be securely disposed
of using NIST SP 800-88 compliant methods. A certificate of destruction is obtained
and retained for all physical media disposals. Logical deletion of sensitive data
follows documented secure erasure procedures.

=============================================================================
SECTION B — SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
=============================================================================

15. VULNERABILITY MANAGEMENT (TPC-42)

Systems connecting to Saudi Aramco networks are scanned for vulnerabilities using
approved scanning tools on a monthly basis. Critical vulnerabilities (CVSS ≥ 9.0)
must be remediated within 15 days. High vulnerabilities (CVSS 7.0–8.9) must be
remediated within 30 days. Vulnerability scan reports and remediation status are
provided to Saudi Aramco upon request and reviewed with the Saudi Aramco security
team quarterly.

16. PENETRATION TESTING (TPC-43)

Annual penetration testing is conducted by an independent qualified third party.
Scope covers all systems and interfaces that connect to Saudi Aramco. Test findings
are documented, risk-rated, and remediated within defined timelines. Testing results
and remediation evidence are available to Saudi Aramco upon request.

17. SECURITY INCIDENT MANAGEMENT (TPC-60)

A documented incident response plan (IRP) is maintained, tested via tabletop exercises
annually, and updated following every major incident. Security incidents that affect
or potentially affect Saudi Aramco data or systems must be reported to Saudi Aramco's
Cybersecurity Operations Center within 2 hours of detection. Post-incident reviews
are conducted within 5 business days and written reports are shared with Saudi Aramco
including root cause, impact, and corrective actions implemented.

18. SECURE DEVELOPMENT LIFECYCLE (TPC-70)

Software developed for Saudi Aramco follows a documented Secure Development Lifecycle
(SDLC) incorporating threat modeling, security requirements, secure coding standards,
code review, SAST/DAST testing, and security sign-off before release. OWASP Top 10
and SANS Top 25 vulnerabilities are addressed in all custom code. Security testing
results are documented and retained.

19. CLOUD SECURITY (TPC-80)

Cloud services used in the delivery of Saudi Aramco services are configured according
to the CSP's security hardening benchmarks (CIS or equivalent). Data residency
requirements are documented and enforced to restrict Saudi Aramco data to approved
geographic regions. Cloud access control follows least privilege; all cloud administrative
access requires MFA. Cloud security posture is reviewed quarterly using CSPM tooling.

20. SUPPLY CHAIN SECURITY (TPC-85)

All sub-contractors and fourth parties with access to Saudi Aramco data or systems
are subject to the same cybersecurity requirements as the Third Party. A register of
sub-contractors is maintained and reviewed quarterly. Sub-contractor cybersecurity
compliance is assessed annually. Saudi Aramco is notified within 5 business days of
any new sub-contractor engagement or termination.

=============================================================================
COMPLIANCE, AUDIT, AND EXCEPTIONS
=============================================================================

21. COMPLIANCE MONITORING

Compliance with this policy is monitored continuously through automated controls
and reviewed formally by internal audit on an annual basis. Audit findings are
reported to senior management and the CSO within 5 business days. A formal
remediation plan with owners and timelines is produced within 10 business days.

22. EXCEPTION MANAGEMENT

Policy exceptions must be formally requested, risk-assessed, and approved by the
CSO and senior management. All exceptions are time-bounded (maximum 90 days),
documented in the exception register, and reviewed at each renewal. Exceptions
are disclosed to Saudi Aramco upon request.

23. POLICY REVIEW AND UPDATES

This policy is reviewed annually or upon significant changes to the threat landscape,
regulatory requirements, or Saudi Aramco directives. Updates are approved by the CSO
and communicated to all relevant personnel within 10 business days of approval.
Previous policy versions are archived and retained for 5 years.
